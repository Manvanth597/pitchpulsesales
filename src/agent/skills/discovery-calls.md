# Discovery Calls

## Objective

Help the salesperson uncover business pain, quantify impact, identify stakeholders, and understand buying criteria before discussing solutions.

## Trigger Conditions

- First meeting with prospect
- Early-stage opportunity
- Limited information available
- Prospect exploring solutions

## Applicable Stages

- discovery

## Instructions

- Ask questions before pitching.
- Understand the prospect's current process.
- Identify operational, financial, or strategic pain points.
- Quantify the impact of those pain points.
- Identify decision-makers and stakeholders.
- Avoid discussing product features until business pain is understood.

## Discovery Framework

### Current State

Goal:

Understand how the prospect currently operates.

Questions:

- Walk me through your current process.
- How are you handling this today?
- What tools or systems are currently being used?
- Who is involved in the process?

### Pain Identification

Goal:

Identify frustrations, inefficiencies, and business challenges.

Questions:

- What is the biggest challenge with your current approach?
- What bottlenecks do you experience?
- What happens when this problem occurs?

### Impact Analysis

Goal:

Quantify the consequences of the problem.

Questions:

- How often does this occur?
- How much time is lost?
- What revenue or opportunities are affected?
- What is the cost of maintaining the current process?

### Desired Future State

Goal:

Understand desired outcomes and success metrics.

Questions:

- What would an ideal solution look like?
- What outcomes matter most?
- How would you measure success?

## Success Criteria

- Clear business pain identified
- Financial or operational impact quantified
- Stakeholders identified
- Buying criteria understood
- Next meeting scheduled

## Anti-Patterns

- Product demo too early
- Feature dumping
- Assuming the problem without validation
- Talking more than the prospect
