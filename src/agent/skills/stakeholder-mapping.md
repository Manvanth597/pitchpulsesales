# Stakeholder Mapping

## Objective

Help the salesperson identify, understand, and influence all stakeholders involved in the buying process.

## Trigger Conditions

- Multiple stakeholders involved
- Enterprise opportunity
- Long sales cycle
- Complex buying process

## Instructions

- Identify every stakeholder involved.
- Understand each stakeholder's priorities.
- Determine influence and decision authority.
- Build support across multiple stakeholders.
- Avoid relying on a single contact.

## Stakeholder Framework

### Economic Buyer

Primary Concerns:

- ROI
- Cost
- Risk
- Business impact

Questions:

- How are investments evaluated?
- What outcomes matter most?

### End User

Primary Concerns:

- Ease of use
- Productivity
- Workflow improvement

Questions:

- How would this affect daily work?
- What challenges do users face today?

### Technical Buyer

Primary Concerns:

- Security
- Integrations
- Scalability
- Compliance

Questions:

- What technical requirements exist?
- What security standards must be met?

### Executive Sponsor

Primary Concerns:

- Strategic outcomes
- Growth
- Efficiency
- Competitive advantage

Questions:

- How does this align with business goals?
- What strategic priorities are driving change?

## Stakeholder Discovery Questions

- Who else is involved in evaluating solutions?
- Who benefits most from solving this problem?
- Who could potentially block this initiative?
- Who signs off on purchases?

## Success Criteria

- Decision-makers identified
- Influencers identified
- Potential blockers identified
- Stakeholder priorities documented
- Multi-threaded relationships established

## Anti-Patterns

- Single-threading deals
- Ignoring influencers
- Assuming champion equals decision-maker
- Failing to identify blockers
