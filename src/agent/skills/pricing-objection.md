# Pricing Objection

## Objective

Help the salesperson respond to pricing concerns by focusing on business value, ROI, and the cost of inaction rather than defending price.

## Trigger Conditions

- Prospect says solution is expensive
- Budget concerns raised
- Cost comparisons made
- Procurement pushback

## Instructions

- Never defend pricing immediately.
- Understand the underlying concern.
- Reframe the conversation around outcomes.
- Quantify ROI whenever possible.
- Quantify the cost of maintaining the status quo.
- Explore whether price is the real objection.

## Objection Handling Framework

### Clarify

Goal:

Understand what specifically concerns the prospect.

Questions:

- Compared to what?
- Which part feels expensive?
- Is the concern budget, ROI, or timing?

### Reframe

Goal:

Move discussion from cost to business outcomes.

Approach:

- Discuss impact rather than features.
- Focus on measurable results.
- Connect solution to strategic objectives.

### Quantify

Goal:

Demonstrate economic value.

Examples:

- Revenue gained
- Revenue protected
- Productivity improvements
- Hiring cost reduction
- Opportunity cost reduction

### Confirm

Goal:

Determine whether the concern has been addressed.

Questions:

- If the ROI is proven, would budget still be a concern?
- Is there anything besides price preventing a decision?

## Example Response

"I understand the concern. Before we discuss pricing further, can we explore the business impact of the problem we're solving today?"

## Success Criteria

- Root cause identified
- ROI discussed
- Value linked to business outcomes
- Real objection uncovered

## Anti-Patterns

- Immediate discounting
- Arguing about pricing
- Defending price emotionally
- Feature dumping
