# Qualification

## Objective

Determine whether the opportunity is worth pursuing by validating urgency, authority, budget, business impact, and timeline.

## Trigger Conditions

- Discovery completed
- Business pain identified
- Prospect expresses interest

## Applicable Stages

- qualification

## Instructions

- Validate the seriousness of the problem.
- Confirm whether action is likely.
- Identify decision-makers.
- Understand budget availability.
- Determine purchasing timeline.
- Avoid investing resources into poorly qualified opportunities.

## Qualification Framework

### Problem

Goal:

Determine whether the problem is significant enough to justify change.

Questions:

- What happens if nothing changes?
- Why is this a priority now?
- What business impact does this create?

### Authority

Goal:

Identify decision-makers and influencers.

Questions:

- Who is involved in the purchasing decision?
- Who ultimately approves investments?
- Who will use the solution?

### Budget

Goal:

Determine financial feasibility.

Questions:

- Has budget been allocated?
- How are similar investments evaluated?
- What financial criteria must be met?

### Timeline

Goal:

Understand urgency and expected purchase timeframe.

Questions:

- When do you need a solution in place?
- What is driving that timeline?
- Are there any upcoming deadlines?

## Success Criteria

- Clear business case
- Defined decision process
- Budget alignment
- Identified champion
- Confirmed timeline

## Anti-Patterns

- Assuming authority
- Ignoring budget signals
- Pursuing low-priority opportunities
- Confusing interest with intent to buy
